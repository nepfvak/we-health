## The Ancient Art of Needles

Acupuncture has been practiced continuously for over 2,500 years, originating in classical Chinese medicine as a method of restoring balance to the body's vital energy — *qi* (氣). Far from being mysterious, acupuncture is a precise, evidence-informed therapy that works with the body's own regulatory systems.

At W&E Health Institute, we practice acupuncture rooted in classical tradition while drawing on modern anatomical understanding. No two treatments are the same, because no two patients are the same.

## How It Works

The body contains a network of pathways — called *meridians* — through which qi flows. When qi becomes blocked or depleted, symptoms arise: pain, fatigue, digestive upset, emotional imbalance. Acupuncture needles are placed at specific points along these meridians to:

- Release areas of stagnation and tension
- Encourage circulation of blood and lymph
- Regulate the nervous system
- Support the body's natural healing response

The needles are as fine as a strand of hair. Most patients feel little to nothing during insertion, and many fall into a deep relaxation — sometimes called the "acu-nap" — during the treatment.

## What to Expect at Your First Visit

Your first visit begins with a thorough intake: we ask about your chief complaint, your sleep, digestion, emotional state, and any relevant history. We examine your tongue and take your pulse at multiple positions on the wrist — each position reflecting a different organ system.

Treatment typically lasts 60–75 minutes. You'll rest comfortably on the table with needles in place for 20–30 minutes while your body does its work.

Most patients notice a shift — sometimes subtle, sometimes significant — within one to three sessions. Chronic conditions generally require a course of treatment to achieve lasting results.

## Is Acupuncture Right for You?

Acupuncture is remarkably safe and appropriate for a wide range of conditions. We commonly treat:

- Musculoskeletal pain (back, neck, shoulder, knee)
- Headaches and migraines
- Stress, anxiety, and insomnia
- Digestive disorders
- Hormonal and reproductive health
- Post-surgical recovery

If you're curious whether acupuncture could help your specific situation, we're always happy to have a conversation before you book your first appointment.
