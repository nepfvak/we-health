Dr. Yingjuan Liang is a Doctor of Acupuncture and Chinese Medicine (DACM), a Licensed Acupuncturist in Tennessee, and a certified Chinese herbalist board-certified as a Diplomate of Oriental Medicine by the NCCAOM. Raised by parents who were both integrative physicians practicing Western and Chinese medicine, she carries forward a holistic approach: harmonising body and mind, nurturing both form and spirit.

In clinic she integrates modern abdominal diagnosis and tongue diagnosis with Jiao’s Scalp Acupuncture, Abdominal Acupuncture, Master Tung’s Acupuncture, Motion Needle, Five Element, and traditional acupuncture — complemented by herbal prescriptions tailored to the patient in front of her.

We welcome you to experience Dr. Liang’s caring heart and healing hands.

Treatments offered

Eight traditional modalities of classical Chinese medicine, offered alone or in combination. Each first visit begins with a pulse reading and conversation.

Custom formulas of roots, barks, and flowers, decocted or granulated to the constitution — not the diagnosis.

Cupping

Glass cups draw stagnant blood and qi to the surface, freeing tight muscle and clearing channel obstruction.

III. Philosophy 道
氣
qì · vital breath

Acupuncture is not a treatment performed on you. It is a slow conversation with the body — listening for where breath has stopped, where warmth has thinned, where the current of qì has lost its road.

What we practise is older than memory: a medicine of pulse and palpation, of herbs gathered by season, of fine needles placed at the points classical texts have mapped for three millennia. It is patient work. It is meant to be. We do not chase symptoms — we tend to the soil they grew from.
