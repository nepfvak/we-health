Dr. Tieqiang Zhao
MD/PhD (CN) · L.Ac. · NCCAOM Diplomate · Over 20 Years Clinical Experience

Dr. Tieqiang Zhao is a highly accomplished integrative medicine practitioner who seamlessly blends the strengths of Western medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), Tai Chi, and Medical Qigong to provide holistic, personalized care.

Dr. Zhao graduated from the elite 7-year Clinical Medicine program at Harbin Medical University (HMU), one of China's top medical schools. He later completed a 3-year doctoral program, earning both his M.D. and Ph.D. degrees. Before relocating to the United States, he served as an Attending Physician in Internal Medicine at HMU and as an Associate Professor at Peking University Health Science Center, China's leading medical school. In the U.S., he held the position of Assistant Professor in the Department of Internal Medicine at the University of Arizona.

With extensive training in both Eastern and Western medicine, Dr. Zhao completed advanced apprenticeship in Traditional Chinese Medicine under renowned practitioner Alex Li. He also studied Tai Chi with Masters Zhibin Zhang and Songquan Yang, and Medical Qigong with Masters Qiduan Li and Junfeng Li. This unique combination of rigorous medical training and deep mastery of mind-body practices forms the foundation of his integrative healing approach.

Professional Qualifications & Leadership
  • Licensed Acupuncturist in Tennessee
  • Board-Certified Diplomate of Acupuncture (NCCAOM)
  • Certified SAAT Allergy Treatment Provider
  • Over 20 years of clinical experience
  • Executive Director, World Federation of Chinese Medicine Societies – Cardiovascular Disease Committee
  • Professional Member, American Heart Association & American Stroke Association
  • Program Director and Principal Investigator of a NIH-funded research grant
  • National-level Judge of Tai Chi and Kung Fu (USA Wushu Kungfu Federation)
  • Member, The Tai Chi Qigong Association of America
Clinical Philosophy

Years of caring for patients with advanced stages of illness gave Dr. Zhao a profound appreciation for both the power and the limitations of modern medicine. Those experiences strengthened his belief that the best healthcare not only treats illness but also helps prevent it whenever possible.

His approach emphasizes caring for the whole person — body, mind, and spirit — through individualized treatment that integrates the strengths of Western medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Tai Chi, and Medical Qigong.

Every patient is treated with compassion, respect, and genuine dedication, because healing begins with understanding the individual, not just the disease.
