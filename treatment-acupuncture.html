针 Acupuncture

zhēn — Hair-thin sterile needles placed along the body's meridians to release stagnation and restore the flow of qì.

概Overview

The oldest recorded clinical art.

Acupuncture is the insertion of very fine, single-use sterile needles into specific points on the body — points chosen not by symptom alone, but by the full pattern of how a person's system is functioning. The practitioner reads the pulse, observes the tongue, and listens carefully before a single needle is placed. The goal is never to silence a complaint; it is to understand the terrain that allowed it to arise.

Classical Chinese medicine describes the body as a living landscape through which vital energy — qì — moves along established pathways called meridians. When that flow is smooth and adequate, health follows naturally. When it stagnates, scatters, or depletes, symptoms emerge. Fine needles placed at classical points act as a kind of conversation: they prompt the system to notice, to reorganise, to restore its own movement.

Modern research has identified several mechanisms through which acupuncture produces measurable effects — modulation of the autonomic nervous system, local release of neuropeptides, influence on inflammatory markers — but practitioners often observe changes that outrun easy explanation. Both frameworks have value. We hold them together.

What to expect during a session

  • You will lie comfortably on a treatment table while the practitioner selects and inserts a small number of needles — typically 8–20 — along relevant meridian pathways.
  • The sensation is nothing like a standard injection. Most patients feel a mild heaviness, tingling, or warmth at each point — sensations classical texts call the arrival of qì.
  • Needles are retained for 20–40 minutes while you rest. Many patients fall into a light sleep during this phase.
  • Follow-up visits are 60 minutes; an initial first visit at W&E Health Institute is a full 90-minute encounter including pulse reading, tongue photography, and abdominal diagnosis.

Commonly reported benefits

  • Reduction of chronic and acute pain — musculoskeletal, neuropathic, and headache
  • Improved sleep quality and reduced time to sleep onset
  • Decreased anxiety and a calmer, more grounded mood between visits
  • Hormone regulation and improved menstrual regularity
  • Enhanced digestive function and reduced bloating or reflux
  • Greater energy and reduced sense of depletion through the day
