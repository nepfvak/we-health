概Overview

Needles, points, and whole-person care.

Acupuncture is a treatment in which very thin, sterile needles are inserted into specific points on the body. It is commonly used in traditional East Asian medicine and is often sought for pain relief, stress management, headaches, nausea, and general wellness. Many patients describe it as relaxing, and some feel improvement after just a few sessions.

Possible benefits may include reduced pain, improved mobility, decreased muscle tension, and a sense of calm or better sleep. Research suggests acupuncture may help certain conditions, especially some types of chronic pain, although results vary from person to person. It is not a cure-all, but it can be a useful part of a broader care plan.

Most people tolerate acupuncture well. Common side effects are mild and may include temporary soreness, slight bleeding, bruising, or dizziness. Patients should seek treatment from a properly trained, licensed provider who uses sterile, single-use needles.

What to expect during a session

  • You'll lie comfortably while the practitioner inserts a small number of thin, sterile needles at selected points on the body.
  • Most people feel little to no pain; some notice a mild heaviness, tingling, or warmth at the needle site.
  • Needles are typically retained for 20–40 minutes while you rest quietly.
  • An initial visit includes a full intake — pulse reading, health history, and discussion of your goals — before any needles are placed.

Commonly reported benefits

  • Reduced pain, including chronic musculoskeletal pain and headaches
  • Improved mobility and decreased muscle tension
  • A sense of calm, relaxation, or better sleep
  • Support for stress management and general wellness
  • Can be part of a broader, integrative care plan

百會

Located at the top of the head, on the midline between the two ear apices.

Commonly used for: Mental clarity, dizziness, and headaches. Also used to lift the spirit and support focus.

GB20
Fengchi
風池

Located at the base of the skull, in the hollow between the two large neck muscles.

Commonly used for: Headaches, neck tension, colds, and eye strain.

PC6
Neiguan
內關

Located on the inner wrist, two finger-widths above the wrist crease between the two tendons.

Commonly used for: Nausea, anxiety, and heart palpitations.

LI4
Hegu
合谷

Located on the hand, in the fleshy webbing between the thumb and index finger.

Commonly used for: Headaches, facial pain, and immune support.

ST36
Zusanli
足三里

Located below the knee, one finger-width to the outside of the shin bone.

Commonly used for: Digestion, fatigue, and immune support. One of the most widely used points in all of Chinese medicine.

SP6
Sanyinjiao
三陰交

Located above the inner ankle, three finger-widths above the tip of the medial malleolus.

Commonly used for: Menstrual health, insomnia, and digestion.
