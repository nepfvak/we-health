概Overview

A pharmacy shaped to one person.

Herbal medicine uses plant-based formulas such as roots, bark, leaves, flowers, and seeds to support health and treat symptoms. In traditional Chinese medicine, herbs are often combined into individualized formulas based on the patient's overall pattern, rather than on a single diagnosis alone.

Patients may use herbal medicine for digestion, energy, sleep, stress, immune support, or chronic symptoms. Some formulas may help with specific complaints, but the effects depend on the formula, the condition being treated, and the quality of the herbs. Herbal medicine is often best used as part of a broader treatment plan.

Herbs can interact with prescription medications and may not be safe during pregnancy, breastfeeding, or for people with liver, kidney, or bleeding problems. Patients should always tell their clinician about every supplement or herb they are taking. Quality matters, so herbal products should come from trusted sources.

What to expect when prescribed herbs

  • Your practitioner will ask about your symptoms, constitution, and any medications before recommending a formula.
  • Formulas are typically taken as teas, granules, or capsules — your provider will advise on the best form for you.
  • Effects may build gradually over days or weeks; some acute formulas work more quickly.
  • Follow-up visits allow the formula to be adjusted as your condition changes.

Commonly reported benefits

  • Support for digestion, energy, sleep, and stress
  • Immune and seasonal health support
  • Complementary to acupuncture and other therapies
  • Individualized to your specific pattern, not a one-size-fits-all remedy
  • Can address chronic symptoms over time with consistent use
