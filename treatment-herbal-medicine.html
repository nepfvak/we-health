草 Herbal Medicine

cǎo — Custom formulas of roots, barks, and flowers, decocted or granulated to the constitution — not the diagnosis.

概Overview

A pharmacy shaped to one person.

Chinese herbal medicine is an encyclopaedic tradition with a written record spanning more than two thousand years. Its foundational text, the Shennong Bencao Jing, catalogued over 365 substances — roots, bark, seeds, minerals, and leaves — each characterised by its temperature, flavour, and affinity for specific organ systems and channels. Modern practice draws on this lineage while adding centuries of refinement and, increasingly, pharmacological research.

The key distinction from most Western supplement use is that Chinese herbal prescriptions are formulas — combinations of anywhere from four to twenty or more individual substances, each selected not just for its primary effect but for its role in modifying the actions of others in the group. A skilled herbalist constructs a formula the way a composer writes for an ensemble: the whole does something none of its parts could do alone.

At W&E Health Institute, herbal prescriptions are individualised at each visit. Dr. Selina does not use fixed-formula products off a shelf. She reads your current presentation — pulse, tongue, symptoms, season, emotional state — and prepares or adjusts a formula accordingly. Granule concentrates are available for convenience; raw decoctions remain an option for patients whose patterns warrant the deeper action of a full traditional preparation.

What to expect when prescribed herbs

  • Following your initial assessment, Dr. Selina will discuss whether herbal medicine is appropriate alongside or between acupuncture treatments, and answer any questions about ingredients, sourcing, or safety.
  • Granule formulas are the most common delivery: powders dissolved in warm water and taken one to three times daily. They are convenient and have a shelf life of several months.
  • Your formula will be reviewed and adjusted at each follow-up visit as your pattern changes — herbal prescriptions are never static over a course of treatment.
  • Please bring a current list of any Western medications you take so potential interactions can be assessed before a formula is prescribed.

Commonly reported benefits

  • Sustained improvement in conditions that acupuncture alone addresses only partially — providing daily therapeutic action between clinic visits
  • Support for hormonal irregularity, fertility, and the menstrual cycle
  • Improvement in digestive and metabolic function, including bloating, reflux, and sluggish bowel
  • Reduction of systemic inflammation and support during recovery from illness
  • Calming of anxiety and support for more restful, unbroken sleep
  • Gradual strengthening of constitutional deficiencies — the kind of deep fatigue that short-term treatment rarely reaches
