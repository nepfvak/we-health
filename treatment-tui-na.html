概Overview

Therapeutic bodywork guided by tradition.

Tui Na is a form of therapeutic Chinese massage that uses pressing, kneading, rolling, stretching, and other hands-on techniques. It is often used to address muscle tightness, joint stiffness, pain, and limited range of motion. Unlike relaxation massage, tui na is usually more targeted and based on traditional Chinese medicine principles.

Patients may benefit from improved mobility, reduced discomfort, and a feeling of release in tense areas. It is often combined with acupuncture, herbs, or other therapies. Some people also use tui na to support recovery from overuse or chronic tension.

Tui na can cause temporary soreness, especially if the treatment is deep or focused on tight areas. It may not be appropriate for people with fractures, acute injuries, blood clotting disorders, or certain skin conditions. Patients should speak up during treatment if pressure feels too strong.

What to expect during a session

  • The practitioner uses their hands, thumbs, elbows, and forearms to press, knead, and stretch specific areas.
  • You may remain clothed or use draping depending on the treatment area.
  • Pressure can range from gentle to firm — let your practitioner know your preference.
  • Some soreness the day after a deep session is normal, similar to after a workout.

Commonly reported benefits

  • Improved mobility and range of motion in stiff joints
  • Relief from chronic muscle tension and pain
  • Recovery support from overuse or repetitive strain
  • Often combined with acupuncture for a more complete treatment
  • Can address the underlying patterns, not just surface symptoms
